PATTERN RECOGNITION GAME - 100 CLINICAL QUESTIONS

Game Rules:
- Total Questions: 100
- Time Limit: 5 minutes (300 seconds)
- Max Score: 100
- Format: Multiple choice with 4 options each
- Difficulty Levels: Easy, Medium, Hard

Here are the first 30 questions as examples:

EASY QUESTIONS (1-10):

1. Ankle - Soccer player with inversion injury
   Q: 22-year-old soccer player with immediate lateral ankle pain after inversion injury. Tender over ATFL, positive anterior drawer test.
   Options: Lateral ankle sprain, Achilles tendinopathy, Peroneal tendon subluxation, Ankle fracture
   Answer: Lateral ankle sprain

2. Shoulder - Office worker with overhead pain
   Q: 45-year-old office worker with shoulder pain during overhead activities. Painful arc 60-120°, positive Hawkins test.
   Options: Subacromial impingement, Rotator cuff tear, Frozen shoulder, AC joint arthritis
   Answer: Subacromial impingement

3. Foot - Runner with heel pain
   Q: 35-year-old runner with morning heel pain, worse after rest. Point tenderness at medial calcaneal tuberosity.
   Options: Plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendinopathy, Calcaneal stress fracture, Tarsal tunnel syndrome
   Answer: Plantar fasciitis

4. Elbow - Tennis player
   Q: 28-year-old tennis player with lateral elbow pain. Tenderness over lateral epicondyle, pain with resisted wrist extension.
   Options: Lateral epicondylitis, Medial epicondylitis, Radial tunnel syndrome, Cubital tunnel syndrome
   Answer: Lateral epicondylitis

5. Knee - Student with anterior pain
   Q: 19-year-old student with anterior knee pain worse going down stairs and after prolonged sitting. Positive J-sign.
   Options: Patellofemoral pain syndrome, Patellar tendinopathy, IT band syndrome, Meniscus tear
   Answer: Patellofemoral pain syndrome

6. Wrist - Numbness and tingling
   Q: 50-year-old with numbness in thumb and first two fingers, worse at night. Positive Tinel's sign at wrist.
   Options: Carpal tunnel syndrome, De Quervain's tenosynovitis, Trigger finger, Cubital tunnel syndrome
   Answer: Carpal tunnel syndrome

7. Back - Weightlifter injury
   Q: 25-year-old weightlifter with acute lower back pain after deadlifting. Pain with forward flexion, no neurological signs.
   Options: Mechanical low back pain, Disc herniation, Spinal stenosis, Spondylolisthesis
   Answer: Mechanical low back pain

8. Hip - Elderly with stiffness
   Q: 65-year-old with gradual hip pain and morning stiffness <30 minutes. Reduced internal rotation, crepitus.
   Options: Hip osteoarthritis, Hip flexor strain, Labral tear, Trochanteric bursitis
   Answer: Hip osteoarthritis

9. Neck - MVA patient
   Q: 42-year-old after MVA with neck pain and headaches. Reduced cervical ROM, upper trap spasm.
   Options: Whiplash-associated disorder, Cervical radiculopathy, Cervical myelopathy, Torticollis
   Answer: Whiplash-associated disorder

10. Calf - Runner injury
    Q: 40-year-old runner with sudden calf pain during sprint. Palpable defect in medial gastrocnemius.
    Options: Calf muscle strain, Achilles tendinopathy, DVT, Compartment syndrome
    Answer: Calf muscle strain

MEDIUM QUESTIONS (11-20):

11. Knee - Middle-aged with catching
    Q: 55-year-old with knee pain and catching sensation. Joint line tenderness, positive McMurray test, mild effusion.
    Answer: Meniscal tear

12. Back - Radicular symptoms
    Q: 38-year-old with lower back pain radiating to leg. Positive SLR at 45°, weakness in big toe extension.
    Answer: L5 radiculopathy

13. Hip - Basketball player groin pain
    Q: 29-year-old basketball player with groin pain. Pain with resisted hip adduction, positive squeeze test.
    Answer: Adductor strain

14. Hand - Symmetrical joint swelling
    Q: 52-year-old with symmetrical hand joint swelling in MCPs and PIPs. Morning stiffness >1 hour.
    Answer: Rheumatoid arthritis

15. Hip - Runner with lateral pain
    Q: 44-year-old runner with lateral hip pain when lying on side. Positive Trendelenburg test.
    Answer: Greater trochanteric pain syndrome
